# Audit Review

## Domain Reviewed
1.3 Account Lock Manager Race Condition Audit

## Scope Validation
The auditor correctly identified all relevant files bridging the lock acquisition flow: `AccountLockManager.mjs`, `AutomationController.mjs`, `WorkflowEngine.mjs`, and `CashoutWorkflow.mjs`.

## Evidence Validation
**Finding 1 (Severity: Critical)**: The auditor cited `Code E1` and `Static Analysis E4`. However, `audit_evidence_standards.md` strictly dictates that a `Critical` severity finding **MUST** include at least one runtime or experimental evidence category (`E2` or `E3`). The auditor failed to provide an execution trace or runtime telemetry demonstrating the TOCTOU race condition.
**Finding 2 (Severity: High)**: The auditor cited only `Code E1`. The standard requires at least TWO evidence categories for `High` severity. This fails the standard.
**Finding 3 (Severity: Medium)**: The auditor cited `Code E1`, which satisfies the requirement for a `Medium` severity finding.

*Conclusion*: The audit violates the `audit_evidence_standards.md` for both its Critical and High findings. The Reviewer is required to mandate revisions.

## Strengths
- Excellent identification of the cross-boundary Check-Then-Act (TOCTOU) race condition between the Controller and the Workflow.
- Correctly diagnosed the destructive nature of the unconditional `releaseLock` in the `finally` block overlapping with concurrent acquisitions.
- Strong root cause analysis regarding the timeout TTL expiration.

## Weaknesses
- Strict failure to adhere to the mandatory evidence standards for Critical and High findings.

## Missing Analysis
- The auditor did not explicitly mention that the `WorkflowEngine` and `CashoutWorkflow` never update the `BrowserStateRegistry` to mark the browser as `Busy` during execution. If they did, `UNIQUE_ACCOUNTS_ONLY` would at least mitigate sequential duplicate routing, but because the state remains `Ready`, the door is wide open for overlapping command injections.

None. The conclusions are structurally sound and verifiable via the codebase, but lack the mandated execution proofs.

## Required Revisions
1. **Missing Area**: Finding 1 Evidence standard.
   - **Reason**: `Critical` findings must have E2 (Execution Trace) or E3 (Runtime Behavior).
   - **Required Analysis**: Construct a detailed E2 Execution Trace demonstrating the exact sequence of events across the asynchronous boundaries where the lock is verified but not acquired.
   - **Priority**: Critical

2. **Missing Area**: Finding 2 Evidence standard.
   - **Reason**: `High` findings must have two evidence categories.
   - **Required Analysis**: Provide E4 (Static Analysis) or E2 (Execution Trace) demonstrating how the `finally` block destroys a concurrently held lock.

   - **Reason**: The audit omits analysis of why `UNIQUE_ACCOUNTS_ONLY` fails to protect against this.
